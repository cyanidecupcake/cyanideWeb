Hello students, I hope you had a great day in class today. Throughout the week Seth and I will be posting your projects on the Scratch website, you can find them on this page under "gallery". On this website you can also find tutorials to expand your knowledge of Scratch and make even better and cooler games long after class ends as well as a direct link to the Scratch website!


Today we learned the steps needed to create a video game:

  1. Imagine
  2. Prototype
  3. Create
  4. Play Test
  5. Revise
  6. Play Test
  7. Finished Product


We also learned some new vocabulary words:

  • Sprite - A character, like in a story or a game
  • Stage - Much like a real stage, you can put different sets or backgrounds onto the stage
  • Background - The image that the stage wears
  • Script - An action or command that tells the sprite or stage what to do
  • Costume - Is like your sprite putting on different clothes or a new body


We learned how to make a sprite walk using the arrow keys, all about the x and y axis, what a variable is, and creating games with a clear end goal!
